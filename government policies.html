Government Policies on Rainwater Harvesting

Rainwater harvesting (RWH) is a critical strategy to address India's growing water scarcity and promote sustainable water management. The government has implemented several policies, mandates, and support programs to encourage the adoption of rainwater harvesting systems across the country.

National Water Policy (2012)

The National Water Policy emphasizes rainwater harvesting as a tool to increase water availability, especially in urban and industrial areas. It incentivizes the revival of traditional water harvesting systems and promotes integration of RWH in groundwater recharge efforts.

State-Level Responsibilities

Water is primarily a state subject, so individual states are responsible for enacting laws and regulations supporting rainwater harvesting. The central government provides assistance through schemes and funding programs to complement state actions.

Mandatory Rainwater Harvesting in Building Codes

The Model Building Bye Laws (MBBL) 2016, adopted by most states, mandate the installation of rainwater harvesting systems in new building constructions to ensure sustainable water use.

Key Government Schemes

  • Jal Shakti Abhiyan 2.0: Focuses on water conservation in over 100 water-stressed districts, promoting rainwater harvesting with financial and technical support.
  • Atal Bhujal Yojana: Supports groundwater management, funding community rainwater harvesting projects.
  • Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (MGNREGA): Encourages local construction of ponds, check dams, and rooftop rainwater harvesting installations.
  • State-Specific Initiatives:
    • Tamil Nadu's mandatory rainwater harvesting in all new buildings
    • Rajasthan’s Mukhyamantri Jal Swavlamban Abhiyan (MJSA) reviving traditional and modern RWH schemes
    • Odisha’s CHHATA scheme promoting rooftop collection and recharge wells

Central Ground Water Authority Norms

The Central Ground Water Authority requires rooftop rainwater harvesting systems in new projects to obtain groundwater abstraction permits, promoting sustainable groundwater use.

Financial Incentives and Subsidies

The government offers subsidies up to 40% for installing rainwater harvesting systems, especially in urban centers. Farmers benefit from schemes like farm pond subsidies under the "Per Drop More Crop" initiative to improve irrigation efficiency.

Recent Legislative Efforts

The Rainwater Harvesting in Government Establishments and Schools Bill, 2024 mandates rainwater harvesting in all government buildings and schools, promoting sustainable water use from early education and providing incentives for technological innovation.

Compliance and Penalties

Various local regulations impose penalties for non-compliance with mandatory rainwater harvesting to ensure adherence and promote widespread adoption.

Examples of State and City-Level Mandates

Delhi and surrounding regions require mandatory rainwater harvesting systems in new constructions. Incentives such as property tax rebates and reduced water bills encourage compliance among residents and businesses.

How You Can Benefit

By adopting rainwater harvesting systems aligned with government policies, individuals and institutions can contribute to water sustainability, reduce water bills, and access financial assistance to offset installation costs.

This comprehensive overview of government policies will help visitors understand the regulatory landscape, available support, and the importance of rainwater harvesting in India’s water security efforts.
